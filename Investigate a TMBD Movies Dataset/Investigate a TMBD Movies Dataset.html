Introduction¶

Movies have been always been one of the most popular entertainments in our life. With the development of the film making techniques, the film industry has grown more vigorously than ever before.

Here, I explored the 'TMDb-Moveis' dataset. This dataset contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue. In the later analysis process, I focus on the following two main questions.

1. What features are associated to the movies that have high revenues ?

2. What are the popularity trends of different movie genres from year to year ?

3. How the movie genres and renuves relate?

genres = set(df.genres.apply(lambda x:str(x).split('|')).sum()) #find out all the movies genres in our dataset. 

df_genre = pd.DataFrame() #creat an empty DataFrame.

for genre in genres:
    mask = df.genres.apply(lambda x : genre in str(x).split('|')) #create a new dataframe for each genre
    onedf = df[mask].assign(new_genre = genre)                    # add a column 'new_genre' filled up by the same value
    df_genre = df_genre.append(onedf,ignore_index = True)         # append all the new dataframes together

import warnings
warnings.filterwarnings("ignore")

# Initialize the figure
plt.style.use('seaborn-darkgrid')
plt.figure(figsize=(12,8))
 
# create a color palette
palette = plt.get_cmap('Set1')
 
# multiple line plot
num=0
for column in df_popularity.drop('release_year', axis=1):
    num+=1
 
    # Find the right spot on the plot
    plt.subplot(3,3, num)
    
    # plot every groups, but discreet
    for v in df_popularity.drop('release_year', axis=1):
        plt.plot(df_popularity['release_year'], df_popularity[v], marker='', color='grey', linewidth=0.6, alpha=0.3)

 
    # Plot the lineplot
    
    plt.plot(df_popularity['release_year'], df_popularity[column], marker='', color=palette(1.*num/9), linewidth=1.9, alpha=0.9, label=column)
 
    # Same limits for everybody!
    plt.xlim(1970,2015) # x-axis is the years
    plt.ylim(0,max_popularity) # y-axis is the popularity
 
    # Not ticks everywhere
    if num in range(7) :
        plt.tick_params(labelbottom='off')
    if num not in [1,4,7] :
        plt.tick_params(labelleft='off')
 
    # Add title
    plt.title(column, loc='left', fontsize=12, fontweight=0, color=palette(1.*num/9) )
 
# general title
plt.suptitle("The Propularity Trends of \nthe most popular movie genres.", fontsize=14, fontweight=0, color='black', style='italic', y=1);
 
# Axis title
plt.text(1940, -0.7, 'Year', ha='center', va='center')
plt.text(1855, 4, 'Popularity', ha='center', va='center', rotation='vertical')

As shown in the above plot, 'Family' and 'War' are the most popular movie genres in the early 1970s; 'Action' and 'Science Fiction' and 'Adventure' are the most popular movie genres in the mid 1970s; 'War' and 'Thriller' are the most popular movie genres in 1980s; 'Animation' is the most popular movie genre in 1990s; 'Fantasy' is the most popular movie genre in 2000s; after 2010, the popularity of all the movie geners is increasing, and 'Adventure' is the most popular movie genre.

Conclusions¶

The total movie revenues have a rising trends from year by year. The movies that have high revenues are mostly released in the Summer and Decembre. The movies that have genres of 'Adventure', 'Science Fiction' and 'Fantansy' have the highest revenues on average. There are only small portion of movies have extremely extremely high revenues or extremely high popularity. And there is no strongly corelation between the movies popularity and the movies revenues. The most popular movies genres have beening changing from time to time. Recently, the popularity of the most movie genres is constantly increasing. And the growth of the popularity for the genres of 'Adventure' and 'Science Fiction' are significant.

Although we draw several conlusions for the above analysis process, after cleaning the NaN and zero values, this dataset is too small to be an effective representation of the population. Therefore, thoses conclusions stated above are not of pratical significance to the film industry.
